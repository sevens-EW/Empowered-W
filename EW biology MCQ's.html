MCQ's:

  1. A Pair of corresponding chromosomes of the same shape, size and one from each parent is called:
    1. Autosomes
    2. Allosomes
    3. Analogous chromosomes
    4. Homologous chromosomes
  2. During a practical exam, a plant cell in a particular solution was placed under a compound microscope. Students were told to observe the cell and name the tonicity of the solution and mention the process that occurred in the cell.
    1. Isotonic, Endosmosis
    2. Hypotonic, Active Transport
    3. Hypertonic, Endosmosis
    4. Hypertonic, Exosmosis
  3. Who among the following is called the "father of genetics"?
    1. Mendel
    2. Watson and Crick
    3. Darwin
    4. Lamarck
  4. The 9 : 3: 3 :1 ratio dihybrid is due to:
    1. Segregation
    2. Crossing over
    3. Independent assortment
    4. Homologous pairing
  5. Transpiration pull will be maximum under which of the following conditions?
    1. Open stomata, dry atmosphere and moist soil
    2. Open stomata, high humid atmosphere and well irrigated soil
    3. Open stomata, high humid atmosphere and dry soil
    4. Close stomata, dry atmosphere and dry soil
  6. Which one of the following would NOT be a limiting factor for photosynthesis?
    1. Oxygen
    2. Light
    3. Carbon dioxide
    4. Chlorophyll
  7. Ethylene is used for:
    1. Retarding ripening of tomatoes
    2. Hastening of ripening of fruits
    3. Slowing down ripening of apples
    4. Both (b) and (c)
  8. What is the chief nitrogenous waste in mammals?
    1. Amino acid
    2. Uric acid
    3. Ammonia
    4. Urea
  9. Which of the following glands has both endocrine and exocrine functions?
    1. Pituitary gland
    2. Thyroid gland
    3. Pancreas gland
    4. Adrenal gland
  10. During a study session about evolution, one of your fellow students remarks, "The giraffe stretched its neck while reaching for higher leaves; its offspring inherited longer necks as a result". To correct your friend's misconception, what would you say?
    1. Spontaneous mutations can result in the appearance of new traits.
    2. Only favorable adaptations have survival value
    3. Overproduction of offspring leads to a struggle for survival.
    4. Characteristics acquired during an organism's life are not passed on through genes.
  11. Vasopressin is concerned with:
    1. Quick digestion
    2. Slow heart beat
    3. Concentration of urine
    4. Dilution of urine
  12. When cell is fully turgid, which of the following will be zero?
    1. Osmotic pressure
    2. Turgor pressure
    3. Wall pressure
    4. Suction pressure (SP) or Diffusion Pressure Deficit.
  13. A plant is kept in a dark cupboard for about 48 hours before conducting any experiment on photosynthesis to:
    1. Remove starch from the plant
    2. Ensure that starch is not translocated from the leaves
    3. Remove chlorophyll from the leaf of the plant
    4. Remove starch from the experimental leaf
  14. Which one of the following is a greenhouse gas?
    1. Oxygen
    2. Methane
    3. Sulphur dioxide
    4. Nitrogen
  15. Lamarckism cannot explain:
    1. Webbed toes in aquatic birds
    2. Weak muscles in the son of a wrestler
    3. Long, narrow and limbless body of snakes
    4. Heterophylly

2. (d)
3. (a)
4. (c)
5. (a)
6. (a)
